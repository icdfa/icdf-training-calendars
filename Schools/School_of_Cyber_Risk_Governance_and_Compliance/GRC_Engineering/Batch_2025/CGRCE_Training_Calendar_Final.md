## Program Overview

The Certified GRC Engineer (CGRCE) program is a comprehensive 12-month intensive training designed by the International Cybersecurity and Digital Forensics Academy (ICDFA). The program consists of three phases covering compliance foundations, advanced GRC integration, and practical implementation with automation.

### Assessment Structure

Each phase concludes with a **Competency Examination** comprising three papers:

- **Paper 1 (Objective):** Multiple-choice and short-answer questions testing theoretical knowledge
- **Paper 2 (Practical):** Hands-on lab-based challenges demonstrating technical proficiency
- **Paper 3 (Oral/Professional Discussion):** Scenario-based viva voce assessing communication and decision-making skills

Minimum cumulative score of **70%** is required to pass the program and earn CGRCE certification.

## Assessment and Grading Structure

### Continuous Assessment (40% of Final Grade)
- Weekly assignments and quizzes
- Hands-on technical lab exercises and reports
- Participation in live sessions and workshops
- Engagement in case studies and simulations

### Phase Competency Examinations (30% of Final Grade)
- Phase 1 Competency Exam (10%)
- Phase 2 Competency Exam (10%)
- Phase 3 Competency Exam (10%)

Each examination comprises three papers with equal weighting:
- Paper 1 (Objective): 33.3%
- Paper 2 (Practical): 33.3%
- Paper 3 (Oral/Professional Discussion): 33.3%

### Capstone Project (30% of Final Grade)
- Project Submission: Complete report, code, and documentation (15%)
- Final Defense: Presentation and Q&A before industry panel (15%)

**Minimum Passing Score:** 70% cumulative

## Important Notes

1. **Weekend-Only Schedule:** All training sessions are conducted on Saturdays and Sundays to accommodate working professionals.

2. **Breaks and Holidays:** The calendar includes mid-term breaks after Phase I and Phase II, and a December break. Additional holidays may be observed as per institutional policy.

3. **Flexibility:** Dates may be adjusted based on institutional holidays, weather conditions, or unforeseen circumstances. Participants will be notified in advance of any schedule changes.

4. **Capstone Project:** The capstone project is a significant component of Phase III and requires substantial independent work. Participants are encouraged to start planning their projects early.

5. **Certification Eligibility:** Participants must complete all phases, pass all three competency examinations with a minimum score of 70%, and successfully defend their capstone project to be eligible for the CGRCE credential.

6. **Continuous Learning:** The GRC field is rapidly evolving. Graduates are encouraged to pursue continuous professional development and stay updated with emerging trends and technologies.
