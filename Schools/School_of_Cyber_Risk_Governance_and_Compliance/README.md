# School of Cyber Risk, Governance and Compliance (SCRGC)

## Overview

The School of Cyber Risk, Governance and Compliance (SCRGC) at the International Cybersecurity and Digital Forensic Academy (ICDF Academy) provides specialized training in cybersecurity governance, risk management, and regulatory compliance. Our programs prepare professionals to develop, implement, and manage comprehensive cybersecurity governance frameworks.

## Mission

To deliver expert-level training in cybersecurity governance, risk management, and compliance, preparing professionals to lead organizational cybersecurity programs and ensure regulatory compliance.

## Programs Offered

### GRC Engineering (Certified GRC Engineer - CGRCE)

A comprehensive certification program focused on Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) engineering. Participants develop expertise in designing and implementing GRC frameworks, conducting risk assessments, and ensuring compliance with cybersecurity regulations and standards.

**Program Details:**
- **Duration:** Multiple phases (Phase 1, Phase 2, etc.)
- **Format:** Practical training with framework development
- **Delivery:** Intensive courses with compliance projects
- **Certification:** Certified GRC Engineer (CGRCE)

**Current Cohorts:**
- [Batch 2025](GRC_Engineering/Batch_2025/)

## Key Features

### Comprehensive GRC Training
In-depth coverage of governance, risk management, and compliance principles and practices.

### Framework Development
Hands-on experience designing and implementing GRC frameworks for organizations.

### Regulatory Compliance
Training on major cybersecurity regulations and compliance requirements including GDPR, NIST, ISO 27001, and others.

### Risk Management
Advanced techniques for identifying, assessing, and mitigating cybersecurity risks.

### Professional Certification
Program leads to the Certified GRC Engineer (CGRCE) certification.

### Industry-Standard Practices
Training based on industry best practices and recognized frameworks.

## Admission Requirements

Prospective students should have:
- Professional experience in cybersecurity, IT, or risk management
- Understanding of organizational security concepts
- Interest in governance and compliance roles
- Strong analytical and problem-solving skills
- Commitment to professional development
