## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Phase 2 Final Training Calendar outlines a comprehensive eight-week intensive program designed to equip cybercrime investigators with advanced practical skills, forensic analysis capabilities, legal knowledge, and real-world case study experience. This phase builds upon foundational knowledge and provides participants with hands-on training through five integrated case studies, culminating in a capstone project.

## TRAINING SCHEDULE

### Week 1-2: Cyber Forensic Data Analysis
**Course Code:** ACI902  
**Duration:** January 5 - January 16, 2026  
**Unit Credit:** 4 Units  
**Exam Type:** Practical  
**Status:** Core Course  

**Course Overview:**  
This intensive two-week course focuses on the principles and techniques of cyber forensic data analysis. Participants will develop expertise in identifying, preserving, analyzing, and presenting digital evidence in compliance with legal standards. The course covers advanced topics including file system analysis, data recovery techniques, network forensics, and mobile device forensics.

**Key Learning Outcomes:**
- Master digital evidence preservation and chain of custody procedures
- Analyze file systems and recover deleted data
- Conduct network forensic investigations
- Extract and analyze mobile device data
- Document and present forensic findings professionally

### Week 5-6: Cybercrime Investigations Case Studies
**Course Code:** ACI901  
**Duration:** February 2 - February 13, 2026  
**Unit Credit:** 4 Units  
**Exam Type:** Practical  
**Status:** Core Course  

**Course Overview:**  
This course provides an intensive, practical examination of five real-world cybercrime cases. Participants will apply the knowledge and skills acquired in previous courses to analyze complex investigative scenarios, develop investigative strategies, and present findings. Each case study represents a different category of cybercrime and challenges participants to think critically and solve multifaceted problems.

**Key Learning Outcomes:**
- Analyze complex cybercrime scenarios
- Apply forensic techniques to real-world cases
- Develop comprehensive investigative strategies
- Present findings and recommendations professionally
- Integrate legal and ethical considerations into investigations

---

#### CASE STUDY 1: The E-Commerce Data Breach
**Scenario:** A major online retailer experiences a large-scale data breach affecting millions of customer records.

**Investigation Focus:**
- Identify the point of entry and attack vector
- Determine the extent of data loss and compromised systems
- Trace the attacker's lateral movement through the network
- Attribute the attack to threat actors
- Develop recommendations for incident response and prevention

**Skills Applied:** Network forensics, log analysis, malware analysis, threat attribution

---

#### CASE STUDY 2: The Insider Threat
**Scenario:** A corporate employee is suspected of stealing and leaking sensitive company intellectual property.

**Investigation Focus:**
- Identify unauthorized data access and exfiltration
- Analyze user activity logs and file access patterns
- Trace data movement and storage locations
- Establish motive and opportunity
- Develop evidence for legal proceedings

**Skills Applied:** User activity analysis, data exfiltration detection, timeline reconstruction, evidence collection

---

#### CASE STUDY 3: The Ransomware Attack
**Scenario:** A small business falls victim to a ransomware attack, with critical systems encrypted and data held for ransom.

**Investigation Focus:**
- Identify the initial infection vector
- Trace the ransomware propagation through systems
- Analyze the attacker's communication and ransom demands
- Determine recovery options and data restoration
- Develop incident response and business continuity strategies

**Skills Applied:** Malware analysis, system recovery, threat intelligence, incident response

---

#### CASE STUDY 4: The Phishing Campaign
**Scenario:** A sophisticated phishing campaign targets a financial institution, compromising employee credentials and enabling unauthorized access.

**Investigation Focus:**
- Trace the phishing email infrastructure
- Identify the malicious links and attachments
- Attribute the campaign to threat actors
- Determine the scope of credential compromise
- Develop security awareness and prevention measures

**Skills Applied:** Email forensics, infrastructure analysis, threat attribution, social engineering investigation

---

#### CASE STUDY 5: The Dark Web Marketplace
**Scenario:** Law enforcement investigates a dark web marketplace dealing in illegal goods and services.

**Investigation Focus:**
- Deanonymize marketplace operators and users
- Analyze cryptocurrency transactions and money laundering
- Identify hosted infrastructure and hosting providers
- Gather intelligence on illegal activities
- Develop prosecution strategies and evidence packages

**Skills Applied:** Dark web investigation, cryptocurrency analysis, anonymization techniques, intelligence gathering

---

### Week 7-8: Capstone Project in Cybercrime Investigations
**Course Code:** ACI904  
**Duration:** February 16 - February 27, 2026  
**Unit Credit:** 5 Units  
**Exam Type:** Practical  
**Status:** Core Course  

**Course Overview:**  
In this capstone course, participants undertake a comprehensive, simulated cybercrime investigation that integrates all skills and knowledge acquired throughout Phase 2. Working individually or in teams, participants will manage a complex investigation from initial incident response through final reporting and presentation. This course culminates in a professional investigation report and oral presentation to academy faculty and industry experts.

**Key Learning Outcomes:**
- Manage a complete cybercrime investigation lifecycle
- Integrate forensic, legal, and ethical considerations
- Develop professional investigation reports
- Present findings to stakeholders and legal professionals
- Demonstrate mastery of all Phase 2 competencies

**Capstone Project Requirements:**
- Comprehensive incident response and investigation plan
- Detailed forensic analysis and findings documentation
- Legal and ethical compliance assessment
- Professional written report (minimum 50 pages)
- Oral presentation and defense of findings
