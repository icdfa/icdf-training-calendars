# Phase 2: Advanced Practical Training with Case Studies

## Phase Overview

Phase 2 is the advanced phase of the Advanced Cybercrime Investigations program, featuring intensive practical training, real-world case studies, and a comprehensive capstone project. This eight-week phase builds upon the foundational knowledge from Phase 1 and provides participants with expert-level skills in cybercrime investigation and digital forensics.

## Phase Objectives

Upon completion of Phase 2, participants will be able to:

1. Conduct advanced digital forensic analysis using industry-standard tools
2. Apply legal and ethical frameworks to cybercrime investigations
3. Analyze complex cybercrime scenarios and develop investigative strategies
4. Manage comprehensive cybercrime investigations from start to finish
5. Present professional investigation findings suitable for legal proceedings
6. Integrate forensic, legal, and ethical considerations in investigations
7. Demonstrate mastery of advanced cybercrime investigation competencies

## Course Structure

Phase 2 consists of four core courses delivered over eight weeks:

### Week 1-2: Cyber Forensic Data Analysis
**Course Code:** ACI902  
**Units:** 4  
**Exam Type:** Practical

This course focuses on advanced digital forensic analysis techniques, including file system analysis, data recovery, network forensics, and mobile device forensics.

[View Course Details](Course_Schedules/ACI902_Course_Schedule.md)

### Week 3-4: Legal and Ethical Aspects of Cybercrime
**Course Code:** ACI903  
**Units:** 4  
**Exam Type:** Practical

This course covers national and international cybercrime laws, privacy regulations, evidence admissibility, and ethical responsibilities of investigators.

[View Course Details](Course_Schedules/ACI903_Course_Schedule.md)

### Week 5-6: Cybercrime Investigations Case Studies
**Course Code:** ACI901  
**Units:** 4  
**Exam Type:** Practical

This course provides in-depth analysis of five real-world cybercrime cases, with practical application of investigative techniques.

[View Course Details](Course_Schedules/ACI901_Course_Schedule.md)

### Week 7-8: Capstone Project in Cybercrime Investigations
**Course Code:** ACI904  
**Units:** 5  
**Exam Type:** Practical

This course culminates in a comprehensive capstone project integrating all skills and knowledge from Phase 2.

[View Course Details](Course_Schedules/ACI904_Course_Schedule.md)

Phase 2 features five integrated case studies providing practical application of investigative techniques:

## Instructional Approach

Phase 2 utilizes a comprehensive instructional approach:

### Practical Hands-On Training
Laboratory exercises and simulations using industry-standard forensic tools and real-world scenarios.

### Case Study Analysis
In-depth examination of actual cybercrime cases with guided investigation and analysis.

### Expert Instruction
Courses taught by experienced cybersecurity and forensic professionals with field expertise.

### Peer Collaboration
Group discussions, team projects, and collaborative problem-solving activities.

### Professional Development
Focus on developing professional documentation, reporting, and presentation skills.

## Assessment and Evaluation

All Phase 2 courses are evaluated through practical examinations assessing:

- Technical proficiency with forensic tools and techniques
- Analytical and problem-solving abilities
- Professional documentation and reporting skills
- Ethical decision-making and legal compliance
- Presentation and communication abilities

## Capstone Project

The capstone project is the culminating experience of Phase 2, requiring participants to:

1. Manage a complete cybercrime investigation lifecycle
2. Integrate forensic, legal, and ethical considerations
3. Develop a professional investigation report (minimum 50 pages)
4. Present findings to faculty and industry experts
5. Demonstrate mastery of all Phase 2 competencies

[View Capstone Project Guidelines](Assessments/ACI904_Capstone_Requirements.md)
